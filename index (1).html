HIV is a retrovirus, meaning that it uses RNA as its genetic material instead of DNA. When HIV enters a CD4+ T-cell, it converts its RNA into DNA using an enzyme called reverse transcriptase

25 Jan 2023 Awareness about HIV and AIDS

HIV is a virus that attacks the immune system, specifically CD4 cells, which are responsible for fighting off infections. If left untreated, HIV can lead to AIDS, a condition that causes the immune system to become so weak that it can no longer fight off infections and diseases.
